LGBTQ+ Terms Glossary

A collection of LGBTQ+ terms and related links for those who may not be familiar with certain vocabulary commonly used by LGBTQ+ individuals or to describe them.
Please refer to the contact page with any feedback on this list. You may wish for a word to be added, edited, removed, etc.


Glossary compiled and edited with terms and descriptions from these established lists:

National LGBT Health Education Center
Queer Dictionary




Agender (adj): Describes a person who identifies as having no gender


Ally (noun): A person who supports and advocates for the rights of LGBTQ+ people


Aromantic (adj): Describes a person who feels little or no romantic attraction to other people and/or has little interest in forming romantic relationships


Asexual (adj): Describes a person who experiences little or no sexual attraction to other people. Asexuality is not the same thing as celibacy, however


Assigned sex at birth (adj): The sex (often male or female) assigned to a child at birth. Most often based on the child's external anatomy (such as the genitalia). Sometimes referred to as birth sex, natal sex, biological sex, or sex.

An excellent article that further explores this concept from Planned Parenthood: Sex and Gender Identity



Bigender (adj): Describes a person whose gender identity is a combination of two genders.


Binding (verb): Tightly wrappng one's chest in order to minimize the appearance of having breasts.


Biphobia (noun): The fear of, discrimination against, or hatred of bisexual people or those who are perceived as being bisexual


Bisexual (adj): A sexual orientation that describes a person who is emotionally and sexually attracted to people of their own gender and people of other genders


Bottom surgery (noun): A colloquial way of describing gender-affirming genital surgery


Cisgender (adj): A person whose gender identity and assigned sex at birth correspond. A non-transgendered person


Coming out (verb): The act of one coming to an acceptance and/or identifying one's own sexual orientation and/or gender identity. This may be personal acceptance and/or the sharing of one's sexual orientation and/or gender identity with others


Cross-sex hormone therapy (noun): The administration of hormones for those who wish to match their physical secondary sex characteristics (sexually dimorphic characteristics not directly involved in reproduction) to their gender identity


Disorders of Sex Development (DSD) (noun): Group of rare conditions where the reproductive organs and genitals do not develop as expected. Includes Klinefelter Syndrome and Androgen Sensitivity Syndrome. See Intersex Drag (verb): The performance of one or multiple genders in a theatrical sense. Some names for such performers are Drag Kings and Drag Queens


Gay (adj): A sexual orientation that describes a person who is emotionally and sexually attracted to people of their own gender. This term can be used regardless of gender identity, but is most commonly used to describe men


Gender affirming surgery (GAS) (noun): Surgery used to modify one's body to better match one's gender identity. Also referred to as sex reassignment surgery (SRS) or gender confirming surgery (GCS)


Gender binary (noun): A societal idea in which there are only two genders, male or female, and that all people must fit strictly into one category or the other


Gender dysphoria (noun): Distress experienced by some people whose gender identity does not match their assigned sex at birth. This can manifest in some ways such as clinically significant distress or impariment in social, occupational, or other signficant areas of functioning. The Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5) has included gender dysphoria as a recognized diagnosis


Gender expression (noun): THe way a person acts, dresses, speaks, or beahves that aligns with a societal label (i.e., feminine, masculine, andogynous, etc.) A person's gender expression may or may not match their assigned sex at birth or gender identity


Gender fluid (adj): Describes a person whose gender identity is not fixed. Said person may feel like a particular gender or mix of genders on some days, and some other gender(s) another day. Gender identity (noun): A person's internal sense of identifying as a particular gender. This may mean male/man, female/woman, neither, or another gender Gender non-conforming (adj): Describes a gender expression that is different from a given society's norms (a common norm is the gender binary of solely male or female)


Gender role (noun): A set of societal norms that dictate which types of behaviors are considered acceptable, appropriate, and/or desirable for a person based on their actual or perceived sex or gender.


Genderqueer (adj): Describes a person whose gender identity falls outside the traditional or societal gender binary. Also called gender variant, gender expansive, among others. Sometimes written as two words (gender queer)


Heteronormativity (noun): An assumption that every person is heterosexual and that heterosexuality is the superior sexuality


Heterosexual (adj): A sexual orientation that describes women who are emotionall and sexually attracted to men and men who are emotionally and sexually attracted to women. Also known as straight or "being straight"


Homophobia (noun): The fear of, discrimination against, or hated of lesbian or gay people and those who are perceived as such


Intersectionality (noun): The idea that identities are influenced and shaped by race, class, ethnicity, sexuality or sexual orientation, gender or gender identity, physical disability, national origin, etc., as well as by the interconnection of all of these characteristics


Intersex (noun): Group of rare conditions where the reproductive organs and genitals do not develop as expected. See Disorders of Sex Development (DSD)


Lesbian (adj., noun): A sexual orientation that describes a woman who is emotionally and sexually attracted to other women


Men who have sex with men/Women who have sex with women (MSM/WSW) (noun): Categories that are often used in research and public health settings to collectively describe those who engage in same-sex sexual behavior, regardless of their sexual orientation. However, people rarely use the terms MSM or WSW to describe themselves


Minority stress (noun): Chronic stress faced by members of stigmatized minority groups. Minority stress is caused by external, objective events and conditions, expectations of such events, the internalization of societal attitudes, and/or the concealment of one's sexual orientation


Outing (verb): Involuntary or unwanted disclosure of another person's sexual orientation and/or gender identity


Pangender (adj): Describes a person whose gender identity is comprised of many genders


Pansexual (adj): A sexual orientation that describes a person who is emotionally and sexually attracted to people regardless of gender


Polyamorous (adj): Describes a person who has or is open to having more than one romantic or sexual relationship at a time, with the knowledge and consent of all involved parties


QPOC (noun): An acronym that stands for Queer Person of Color or Queer People of Color


Queer (adj): An umbrella term used by some to describe people who think of their sexal orientation or gender identity as outside of set societal norms. Some people view the term queer as more fluid and inclusive than "traditional" categories for sexual orientation and gender identity. The word "queer" has a history as a derogatory term, and may not be embraced or used by all members of the LGBTQ+ community


Questioning (adj): Describes an individual who is unsure about or is exploring their own sexual orientation and/or gender identity


Same gender loving (SGL) (adj): A term used as an alternative to the terms gay and lesbian. SGL is more commonly but noy exclusively used by members of the African American/Black community


Same-sex attraction (SSA) (noun): A term that is used to describe the experience of a person who is emotionall and/or sexually attracted to people of the same gender. Individuals using this term may not feel comfortable using "established" terms for sexual orientation (ie., gay, lesbian, bisexual, etc.) for perosn reasons. This term does not indicate a person's sexual behavior


Sexual orientation (noun): How a person characterizes their own emotional and sexual attraction to others


Social stigma (noun): Negative sterotypes and social status of a person or group based on perceived characteristics that separate that person or group from other members of a society


Structural stigma (noun): Societal conditions, policies, and institutional practices that restrict the opportunities, resources, and well-being of certain groups of people


Top surgery (noun): A colloquial way of describing gender-affirming surgery of the chest area


Transman/transgender man/female-tomale (FTM) (noun): A transgender person whose gender identity is male may use these terms to describe themselves. Others will simply use the term "man"


Trans woman/transgender woman/male-to-female (MTF) (noun): A transgender person whose gender identity is female may use these terms to describe themselves. Some will simply use the term "woman"


Transfeminine (adj): Describes people who were assigned male at birth, but identify with femininity to a great degree than with masculinity


Transgender (adj): Describes a person whose gender identity and assigned sex at birth do not match. Also used as an umbrella term to include gender identities outside of male and female. Sometimes abbreviated as trans


Transition (noun): For transgender people, this refers to the process of coming to recognize, accept, and express one's true gender identity. Most often, this refers to the period when a person makes social, legal, and/or medical changes, such as changing their clothing, name, sex designation, and using medical interventions. Sometimes referrred to as a gender affirmation process


Transmasculine (adj): Describes people who were assigned female at birth, but identify with masculinity to a greater extent than with femininity


Transphobia (noun): The fear of, discrimination against, or hatred of transgender or gender non-conforming people or those who are perceived as such


Transsexual (adj): Sometimes used in medical literature or by some transgender people to describe those who have transitioned through medical intervention(s)


Tucking (verb): The process of hiding one's penis with tape, tight shorts, or specially designed undergarments


Two-Spirit (adj): A contemporary term that connects today's experiences of LGBTQ+ Native American and American Indian people with traditions from their respective cultures
